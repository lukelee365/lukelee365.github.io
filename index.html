Descendants



Descendants is an open-world sandbox adventure game made by Unity that explores indirect human interaction and cooperation. The player takes the role of a human character in a boundless world able to explore, build, and destroy objects at will. When the player's character dies, another player will continue the game with a new character inside the same world. In order to survive and improve the human race as a whole, it is crucial that each player helps the next by leaving hints and clues in the world. This is extremely important because there is no text in this game: the language is invented by the players themselves. By creating this complex virtual world, the game hopes to not only create a challenging experience, but also simulate topics in human history, such as language, technology, migration, resource depletion, and many others.

  • Technologies: Unity3D, C#
  • Cooperators: YouHan Guan, Steele Xia

Chasing Star



Chasing Stars is a game that plays itself. The nine robots are sent into space and their mission is to learn to avoid obstacles and collect star dust. Behind the scene, the evolving robots behave according to different conditions that they encounter using genetic algorithm. The platforms are generated with L-system and are regenerated each time a robot complete a level. The stars are a cellular automaton whose neighbourhood correspond to each robot’s state of life and death. It is interesting and entertaining to watch as the robots stumble and learn to conquer unexpected conditions. Cheer on your favourite robot!

  • Technologies: Unity3D, Piskel, C#
  • Cooperators: LaLaine ulit-destajo, MengMei Zhou

Maze(VR)



Maze explores three User Interface (UI) techniques to improve Role Playing Game (RPG) experience in Virtual Reality (VR). These are demonstrated in Maze, which offers a dynamic three dimensional mini map that attempts to relieve motion sickness and to provide information of surroundings more efficiently, differing from traditional RPGs. Its affordance-orientated UI and corresponding natural hand interaction enhance user's immersive feeling, and offers more comfortable game play experience than traditional RPGs.

Blink(VR)



Blink is an immersive experience in mixed reality with LeapMotion and Oculus. The experience begins with the player choosing to "fall asleep" and entering a virtual world representing a dream. The player can choose to explore the dream world with gestures. However this dream soon turns into a nightmare when you discover slimes. To survive from the attack of the slimes, the player needs to wake up by using the LeapMotion quick switch gesture. After waking up, the player can conquer the nightmare by defeating the slimes using the tools found in the dream world.

  • Technologies: Unity3D, Cinema3D, Leap Motion, Oculus Rift
  • Cooperators: Dannie Lu,YouHan Guan,Lalaine, MengMei Zhou

Lexlide



Lexlide is a gravity-based word puzzle game that acts as an entry point to the terms discussed in Matthew Fuller's book, "Software Studies: A Lexicon". By using the Android tablet's built-in accelerometer, the player must slide correct letters into the word box to build the 38 terms from the lexicon. Once the player succeeds in building a word, a game-altering effect will take place based on the meaning of the word. For example, "Analog" results in letters moving uncontrollably due to signal degradation.

  • Technologies: Visual Studio, OpenFrameWork
  • Cooperators: Steele Xia

Magic Box



Magic Box is a sound-sensitive 6x6x6” blue LED cube. It is controlled by an Arduino Diecimila micro-controller and Max/MSP software. It contains 216 LEDs and every individual LED can be located and addressed. The LED cube is programmed for eight different lighting patterns in total. Two of them are programmed through serial communication with Max/MSP which supports sound detection. Through the Max/MSP patch running on computer, the whole system can receive sound signals and transfer them to light signals.

  • Technologies: Arduino
  • Cooperators: YouHan Guan

Birds



This production of The Birds is a creative collaboration between theatre, dance, digital media and music students together with faculty members and guest artists in York University’s School of the Arts, Media, Performance & Design. AMPD’s rising young talent is engaged in all aspects of the show, from concept to execution -- on stage, backstage and behind the scenes. Students are mentored in designing and producing the play, choreographing, performing and directing, stage management and audience development. In bringing The Birds to life, AMPD’s emerging and established artists join forces to create a ground-breaking production of a seminal work of Canadian and Aboriginal theatre.

  • Technologies: Unity, Processing, AfterEffect
  • Cooperators: Digital Meida Tram
